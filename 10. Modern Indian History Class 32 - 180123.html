Akali movement 1920-25 (05:05 PM)

  • The goal of the movement was to take control of Gurudwara from udashi Sikh Mahant who were corrupt and pro-British, for Example, Opposed Ghadarites.
  • Trigger: They decorated General Dyer after Jalianwala bagh in April 1919.
  • Events: 1921-After the Nankana tragedy where hundreds of Akali were killed by a private army of mahants of Nankana Gurudwara, the movement merged into NCM i.e supported the goals and methods of NCM(1920-22).
  • Therefore Punjabi society became part of INM.
  • Result: SGPC was given control of Gurudwaras under the Gurudwara reform act of 1925.
  • Now educated middle class had the leadership of the masses in Punjab.
  • The politicization of Punjab's peasantry- since the movement was a religious issue, therefore, masses participated.
  • And then in 1921, it merged with NCM.
  • The Akali movement, therefore, strengthened NCM and INM.
  • Rural and urban masses both participated.
  • Hindu-Muslim Sikh unity was witnessed as Hindus and Muslims supported the movement.
  • The movement led to the growth of Nationalist sentiments in the princely state of Punjab as they were geographically contiguous and porous borders.
  • Negatives:
  • Masses got religiously charged, which later encouraged communalism.
  • Akalis got split after 1925 into the Kirti Kisan party having Gandhian and leftists.
  • Akali Dal which had communal elements.
  • They formed the Unionist party which had pro-British elements.

  • Finally, there was violence that led to repression in 1921 but the British also brought the Avadh rent amendment act of 1921.

  • Borsad satyagraha, Gujarat 1922-24
  • It was led by Sardar Patel.
  • Method: Satyagraha
  • Grievance: Collusion between police and dacoits plus Government deployed additional forces and for its expenses applied additional taxes.
  • Action: Patel organized villagers into volunteer corps and started a no-tax campaign against the additional tax levied plus volunteers alerted villagers when officials came to seize property to recover the tax.
  • The movement forced the Governor of Bombay to order an inquiry and therefore led to the resolution of grievances.
  • MG ideology opposed only unjust aspects and therefore opposed additional tax in movements where the Goal is Swaraj the principle of Nontaxation without representation applies therefore here we oppose all taxes.
  • Bardoli satyagraha 1928 (07:05 PM) 
  • Grievance: 30% increase in Land revenue.
  • Preparations were done for the CDM phase of NCM since November 1921(Violence, when Princes of whales visited November 1921, led to No CDM at all India levels and only in Bardoli, In the Form of Constructive work which focussed on unity between Ujali paraj/upper caste and kali paraj/Lower caste)
  • Focused on Anti untouchable, Anti Liquor campaign.
  • Events
  • Patel took leadership-ask of peasants to pay Land revenue but only at old rates.
  • Demanded an independent tribunal.
  • Raised awareness via Bardoli Patrika 
  • Organized peasants including women into chhavanis.
  • Which implemented A tax campaign and social boycott of Nonsupporters.
  • Simultaneously MG raised issues in Young India and Nav Jeevan.
  • Then in 1928, PLA members resigned, and then MG reached Bardoli.
  • Finally under pressure, the British set up an Independent tribunal that decided a 6.03% increase in Land revenue as against 30%/.
  • Also, women and peasants of Bardoli gave the title of Sardar to Patel.
  • Plus MG gave dignity by naming kali paraj as Rani paraj which meant the people of the forest.

Passive phase of INC(1922-27) (07:33 PM) 

  • Lack of political activity by INC after NCM i.e No Movement by INC from 1922-27.
  • Swarajist or pro changers like CR Das, Motilal Nehru, MM Malviya, and Lala Lajpat Rai proposed-End or Mend councils resolution at Gaya session 1922.
  • That is Boycott of councils should stop and INC should fight the 1923 elections to CLA.
  • Arguments Will fill the political void after NCM plus fill empty newspapers with propaganda in councils plus force British to bring constitutional reforms that mend the councils or don't allow councils to function that ends the councils(GOI 1919 -adjournment motion power)
  • Plus if People vote then the loss of face for INC plus Non-INC parties will enter councils, If INC does not fight an election.
  • No Changers were Gandhians like Patel, Nehru, Rajendra prasad, and C.Rajagopalachari-Supported continuation of council boycott and Constructive work because-Council entry will lead to neglect of Constructive work important for preventing another chauri chaura.
  • Plus led to the loss of revolutionary zeal.
  • Plus will lead to political corruption.
  • Therefore continue Constructive work and Non-cooperation by boycotting councils and waiting for the relaunch of CDM was suspended in 1922 via Bardoli resolution 1922.
  • End or mend council resolution failed therefore pro changers resigned from the official post of INC(Not INC per se)
  • Plus formed the congress khilafat swaraj party within INC that fought the 1923 election to CLA.
  • MG prevented a surat-like split as MG was a commonly respected leader of both pro changers and No changers.
  • Plus MG maintained deep respect and did not question the Nationalism of Swarajiist.
  • Plus praised Good work in councils. Example: Swarajist demanded a new constitution in the first session of CLA itself.
  • Plus MG took pro-swarajist stand despite this agreement when the British repressed Swarajist in Bengal due to their links with revolutionaries.
  • Example: Revolutionaries campaigned in Election for CR Das.
  • Positives of NO changers
  • a)Constructive work gave work to the party cadre in the passive phase.
  • b)Plus INcrease caste unity plus Rural-urban unity.
  • c)Plus increase membership of INC.
  • d)Plus Boosted Nationalism due to Swadeshi education.
  • e)MG set up his base at Sabarmati ashram in 1917 after releasing from Jail in 1924.
  • f)Here he made charkha famous as a symbol of swadeshi.
  • The announcement of the Simon Commission in 1927 ended the passive phase.
